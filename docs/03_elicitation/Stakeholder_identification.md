## 1. Purpose

This document identifies all potential stakeholders for the e-Bike Rental Startup prior to requirements elicitation. Stakeholder identification is the foundational step in the **BABOK Elicitation & Collaboration** knowledge area, ensuring that no impacted party is overlooked during requirements gathering.

Identifying stakeholders early allows the Business Analyst to:
- Understand the breadth of perspectives and interests involved
- Plan targeted elicitation activities per stakeholder group
- Prioritize engagement based on influence and interest
- Align Agile user stories and epics to real stakeholder needs

---

## 2. Scope

This register covers stakeholders relevant to the **initial metropolitan launch phase** of the e-Bike rental startup. Stakeholders are categorized across four layers:

- **B2C** — Direct end users of the rental service
- **B2B** — Institutional and enterprise clients
- **Operational** — Partners enabling day-to-day service delivery
- **Financial & Strategic** — Stakeholders with investment or expansion interests

---

## 3. Stakeholder Identification Register

### 3.1 B2C — Direct End Users

| Stakeholder | Examples / Context | Interest / Expectation |
|---|---|---|
| **Tourists & Visitors** | Travelers at famous tourist spots, heritage sites, national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and pilgrimage destinations where motorized access is restricted or long walks are required. | Avoid lengthy walks and cover more sightseeing spots efficiently. Access flexible short-term or hourly rentals without fuel concerns. Expect GPS-integrated route guidance and multilingual app support tailored to tourist zones. |
| **General Urban Commuters** | Daily commuters for short-distance travel (1–8 km), occasional riders, students, and residents using e-bikes as a last-mile connectivity solution from transit hubs. | Affordable hourly, daily, and monthly pass options. Conveniently placed docking/charging stations near metro, bus stops, and residential areas. Seamless pickup and drop-off experience via mobile app. |
| **Gig Economy & Delivery Workers** | Food and grocery delivery agents (Zomato, Swiggy, Blinkit), e-commerce last-mile riders operating in high-density restaurant and commercial hubs. | Cost-effective daily or weekly rental subscriptions. Charging port availability near restaurant clusters and dark kitchens. Flexible subscription extension policies based on usage economy and delivery distance. |

---

### 3.2 B2B — Institutional & Enterprise Clients

| Stakeholder | Examples / Context | Interest / Expectation |
|---|---|---|
| **Corporate Employees & Enterprises** | IT parks, SEZs, large campuses (TCS, Infosys, Wipro), organizations with last-mile gap between parking lots or transit stops and office buildings, or large in-campus travel requirements. | Annual or monthly B2B subscription plans with employee-level access management. In-campus fleet allocation for organizations. Fuel savings reports and sustainability metrics for CSR compliance. Priority vehicle availability during shift hours. |
| **Hospitality & Tourism Partners** | Hotels, resorts, Airbnb hosts, travel agencies, and tour operators who wish to offer e-bike rentals as an add-on amenity or curated experience package to their guests. | White-label or co-branded rental tie-ups. Revenue-sharing or referral commission model. Dedicated bike allocation at property premises. Easy booking integration with hotel concierge or OTA platforms. |
| **Government & Municipal Authorities** | Municipal corporations, Smart City Mission teams, state transport departments, traffic police, and environment/urban mobility departments across metropolitan cities. | Compliance with local EV policies, traffic regulations, and public space usage norms. Alignment with Smart City and green mobility goals. Coordination on docking zone approvals, permits, and subsidies. Potential PPP (Public-Private Partnership) collaboration. |

---

### 3.3 Operational & Enabling Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Examples / Context | Interest / Expectation |
|---|---|---|
| **Fleet Maintenance & Operations Partners** | Third-party EV servicing agencies, battery replacement vendors, on-ground operations team handling fleet uptime, charging infrastructure upkeep, and breakdown response. | Clear SLAs for vehicle maintenance turnaround. Reliable inventory and spare parts supply chain. Defined escalation process for fleet downtime. Fair contractual terms and timely payments. |
| **Technology & Platform Partners** | Mobile app development vendors, IoT device providers (GPS trackers, smart locks), payment gateway partners, and cloud infrastructure providers supporting the rental platform. | Clearly defined technical requirements and integration specs. Stable API contracts and versioning. Timely feedback cycles and UAT support. Revenue model clarity (licensing vs. revenue share). |

---

### 3.4 Financial & Strategic Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Examples / Context | Interest / Expectation |
|---|---|---|
| **Investors & Venture Capitalists** | Seed or Series-A investors, angel investors, ESG-focused VCs, and impact investment funds interested in sustainable urban mobility startups. | Clear ROI projections, unit economics, and market expansion roadmap. Transparent financial reporting and milestone tracking. Scalability potential across cities. Alignment with ESG investment mandates. |
| **Channel & Franchise Business Partners** | Local entrepreneurs or franchisees interested in operating e-bike rental hubs in their city or zone under the startup's brand and technology platform. | Attractive franchise model with defined margins. Brand, technology, and training support from the startup. Low operational complexity. Clearly defined territorial rights and expansion criteria. |

## 5. BA Notes & Elicitation Implications

- **Government & Municipal Authorities** and **Investors** are classified as *Critical* priority due to their high influence over business approvals, funding, and regulatory compliance. Early engagement is essential.
- **Tourists & Urban Commuters** are the primary revenue-driving stakeholders. Their requirements will directly inform core product features, pricing models, and UX design.
- **Technology & Platform Partners** must be engaged early in the sprint planning cycle, as their integration requirements will impact the technical backlog significantly.
- **Gig Economy Workers** represent an underserved but high-frequency user segment — their feedback will be critical during elicitation workshops and UAT phases.
- Stakeholder engagement plans, interview guides, and elicitation techniques for each group will be documented in subsequent artifacts under this knowledge area.
