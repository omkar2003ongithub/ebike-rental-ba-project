## 1. Purpose

This document contains structured interview questions designed to elicit requirements from key stakeholder groups for the e-Bike Rental Startup. The interviews are planned as part of the **Elicitation & Collaboration** knowledge area under BABOK v3, with the primary goals of:

- Understanding current transport pain points faced by each stakeholder group
- Identifying feature and service expectations from the rental platform and mobile application

---

## 2. Interview Approach & Guidelines

- **Format:** One-on-one structured interviews (30–45 minutes per session)
- **Question style:** Open-ended, non-leading questions to allow unbiased responses
- Each section begins with a **warm-up question** to establish rapport before probing deeper
- **Follow-up probe questions** are provided to encourage elaboration where needed
- All interviews to be documented and responses mapped to requirements in the RTM
- Stakeholder consent to be obtained before recording any session

### Question Types

| Question Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Warm-up** | Ice-breaker questions to open the conversation and establish context |
| **Pain Point** | Questions targeting current frustrations, gaps, and unmet needs |
| **Feature Expectation** | Questions to surface desired features, capabilities, and service preferences |
| **Closing** | Open-ended wrap-up to capture any insights not covered by structured questions |

## 3. Stakeholder Group 1 — Tourists & Visitors

> **Target:** Domestic & international travelers visiting tourist spots, heritage sites, national parks, and pilgrimage destinations

**Objective:** Understand transport pain points faced by tourists in metropolitan areas and identify platform features that would drive e-bike adoption among travelers.

| # | Question Type | Interview Question | BA Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | Warm-up | Could you briefly describe how you typically get around when visiting a new city or tourist destination? | Establishes baseline transport behaviour and opens the conversation comfortably before probing pain points. |
| Q2 | Pain Point | What are the biggest transportation challenges you face when exploring tourist spots, historical sites, or national parks in a metropolitan city? | Directly surfaces current pain points. Replaces the original double-barrelled question by separating problem identification from solution ideation. |
| Q3 | Pain Point | On average, how much do you spend on local transport — such as cabs, autos, or hired vehicles — during a typical day of sightseeing or local travel? | Captures cost sensitivity data. Refined by removing fixed distance assumptions and keeping it open to the respondent's actual context. |
| Q4 | Feature Expectation | If an e-bike rental service were available at key tourist locations, what features or conveniences would make you choose it over other transport options? | Directly addresses the elicitation goal of identifying app and service feature expectations from the tourist's perspective. |
| Q5 | Closing | Is there anything else about your transport experience as a visitor — positive or negative — that you think would be important for us to know? | Standard BABOK closing question to capture any insights not covered by structured questions. |

**Follow-up Probe Questions:**
- Have you ever used a bike rental or cycle-sharing service while travelling? What was that experience like?
- Would you feel comfortable navigating an unfamiliar city on an e-bike? What would make you more confident?
- How important is the availability of GPS or guided routes when using a rental vehicle in a new city?

## 4. Stakeholder Group 2 — Corporate Employees & Enterprises

> **Target:** Employees at IT parks, SEZs, and large campuses; HR/Admin decision-makers for B2B subscription evaluation

**Objective:** Identify in-campus and last-mile travel pain points for corporate employees and surface feature and policy requirements for a B2B e-bike subscription model.

| # | Question Type | Interview Question | BA Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | Warm-up | Could you describe your typical daily commute — from home to your workplace and any movement within your office campus? | Opens the conversation and establishes the respondent's current commute context without making assumptions about parking or building layout. |
| Q2 | Pain Point | What challenges do you face when travelling between different buildings, zones, or facilities within your organisation's campus or office park? | Broadened to cover all in-campus movement scenarios, not just parking-to-office, avoiding narrow assumptions. |
| Q3 | Pain Point | How much time do you estimate you spend daily on internal campus movement or last-mile connectivity between transit points and your workplace? | Quantifies the time cost of the problem — a key metric for building the business case for corporate e-bike adoption. |
| Q4 | Feature Expectation | If your organisation provided a fleet of e-bikes for campus and last-mile travel, what specific features or policies would make this service genuinely useful for your daily routine? | Neutral, non-leading replacement of original question. Avoids pre-suggesting the solution is beneficial and lets the respondent form their own view. |
| Q5 | Closing | Are there any concerns — safety, practicality, or otherwise — that might prevent you or your colleagues from using an e-bike rental facility at your workplace? | Surfaces adoption barriers and risk factors early — critical for solution design and change management planning. |

**Follow-up Probe Questions:**
- Would a monthly subscription model or a pay-per-ride model suit your usage pattern better?
- How important is it that the e-bike service integrates with your organisation's employee app or access card system?
- Would sustainability metrics — such as fuel saved or carbon offset — influence your or your organisation's decision to adopt this service?

## 5. Stakeholder Group 3 — Business Owners

> **Target:** SME owners, marketing team managers, and franchise/hospitality partners considering e-bike rental for operational use

**Objective:** Understand transport cost burdens and operational challenges for business field teams, and identify the service features and commercial models required for B2B adoption.

| # | Question Type | Interview Question | BA Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | Warm-up | Could you briefly describe the nature of your business and how your team currently handles daily field operations, client visits, or marketing activities? | Establishes business context before probing costs — ensures questions are interpreted correctly across different business types. |
| Q2 | Pain Point | What is the approximate monthly expenditure your organisation incurs on fuel, vehicle maintenance, and travel allowances for field or marketing staff? | Tightened from original — adds vehicle maintenance and travel allowances to fuel, giving a fuller picture of the total cost burden. |
| Q3 | Pain Point | What operational or logistical challenges does your team currently face with transport that impact productivity or business outcomes? | Opens up pain points beyond cost — captures reliability, scheduling, and efficiency issues that pure cost questions miss. |
| Q4 | Feature Expectation | If your organisation were to adopt an e-bike rental subscription for business travel, what service features, billing models, or platform capabilities would you require to make it a viable option? | Neutral future-tense replacement. Avoids the leading assumption that the outcome would be successful and lets the respondent define their own requirements. |
| Q5 | Closing | From a business owner's perspective, what would be the single biggest factor that would make you commit to an e-bike rental partnership for your organisation? | Surfaces the primary decision driver — invaluable for positioning the B2B value proposition and sales strategy. |

**Follow-up Probe Questions:**
- Would you prefer a fixed monthly fleet subscription or a flexible pay-per-use model for your business needs?
- How important is real-time tracking or usage reporting for managing your team's travel on rented vehicles?
- Would co-branding or white-labelling the e-bike service under your organisation's name add value for your business?

## 6. Next Steps

Following completion of stakeholder interviews, the Business Analyst will:

- Compile and categorise all responses by stakeholder group and theme
- Extract functional and non-functional requirements from interview insights
- Map elicited requirements to Epics and User Stories in the Agile backlog
- Document findings in the Requirements Traceability Matrix (RTM)
- Share a summary of findings with the project team for review and validation
